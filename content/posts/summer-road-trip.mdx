# Summer Road Trip Adventures

Sometimes the best trips are the ones where you don't plan too much. We had a rough route mapped out, a playlist that was way too long, and a cooler full of snacks. That was enough.

## The Route

We started in Texas, drove through New Mexico (hello, Carlsbad Caverns!), up into Colorado for some mountain time, across to Utah for those unreal red rock landscapes, and ended in Arizona watching the sunset at the Grand Canyon.

## Favorite Moments

- Pulling over at a random roadside diner in New Mexico that had the best green chile cheeseburger I've ever tasted
- Waking up to a misty morning in a tiny Colorado cabin surrounded by aspens
- That stretch of highway in Utah where the road just disappears into red desert and you feel like you're on another planet

## What I Learned

Road trips teach you patience — with traffic, with each other, with the GPS that keeps trying to send you on dirt roads. But they also teach you to slow down and actually look at what's around you, not just the destination.

## States Checked Off

Five new states colored in on my travel map! The goal of visiting all 50 feels a little more real now.
